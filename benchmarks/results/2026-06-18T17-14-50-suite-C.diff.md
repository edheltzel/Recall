## Root cause — the semantic pass hides real targets behind near-duplicate noise

This is not ranking noise. The semantic pass **auto-marked 5 ground-truth targets as
duplicates of near-duplicate noise records**, removing them from search entirely. Verified by
inspecting the dedup plan at 10k (seed 47):

| hidden target | reason | cosine | survivor kept visible | survivor is a target? |
|---|---|---:|---|---|
| `t_atlas_phoenix` | semantic | 0.983 | `decisions:415` | no — a near-dup |
| `t_glacier_compaction` | semantic | 0.981 | `decisions:459` | no — a near-dup |
| `t_wal_journaling` | semantic | 0.976 | `decisions:127` | no — a near-dup |
| `t_sqlite_busy` | semantic | 0.993 | `learnings:793` | no — a near-dup |
| `t_timeout_ui` | semantic | 0.981 | `learnings:633` | no — a near-dup |

The near-duplicate fixtures are `target.text + " - " + suffix` **with the target's own
`detail` copied verbatim**, so their embeddings sit at 0.97–0.99 cosine to the real target —
above the 0.95 merge threshold. When a near-dup happens to land in the same table *and* project
as its target, the pair becomes actionable, and survivor selection (`compareForSurvivor`,
`src/lib/dedup.ts:117-126`) ranks the pair by **provenance → richness (longer normalized text)
→ importance → recency (newer) → lowest id**. It has **no way to tell the real target from its
noise twin** — and the comparison structurally favors the noise copy: the near-dup is literally
the target's text *plus* a suffix, so whenever provenance ties it is the **longer** text and
wins the richness tiebreaker; and when the near-dup drew a higher-ranked random provenance
(`extracted` is the most common noise provenance, weighted 0.6) it wins outright before
richness is even consulted. Importance is only the *third* key and was not the deciding factor
in the reproduced hidden-target cases — they turned on provenance or longer suffixed text. So
the noise copy survives and the genuine target is marked hidden. The harm scales with corpus
size (more near-dups → more collisions):
inert at 100/1k, 5 targets lost at 10k.

## Why 100k is omitted (computational infeasibility, itself a finding)

The shipped semantic pass (`findSemanticPairs`) is **O(n²)** over embedded candidates, and the
backfill is one sequential Ollama embedding call per record. The 100/1k/10k run took **311 s**
wall-clock (~11,100 embeds + the 10k O(n²) pass). At 100k the backfill alone is ~100k
sequential embeds and the semantic comparison is ~100× the 10k pass — hours, not minutes.
This is not a harness limitation to engineer around; it means **the embedding-backed semantic
pass as it ships does not scale to the corpus size where precision-under-noise is worst** (the
100k rung, where baseline MRR is already 0.00). A drop-in embedding fix for #49 would have to
solve both the O(n²) cost and the false-merge safety problem below before it could even be
measured at 100k.

## Interpretation (honest, no invented thresholds)

The open question in #99 was: *would an embedding-backed semantic dedup pass at 0.95 group the
target-vs-near-duplicate pairs and lift precision-under-noise?* The answer is **no — it lowers
it.**

- **At 100 and 1,000:** completely inert (no actionable target/near-dup collisions; MRR
  identical to both baseline and #78).
- **At 10,000:** **net-negative.** The semantic pass groups the pairs as predicted, but
  survivor selection cannot distinguish a real target from its near-duplicate, so it hides 5
  genuine targets behind noise. MRR drops 0.1095 → 0.0179; exact-lookup MRR 0.1333 → 0.0625;
  R@5 halves.
- **At 100,000:** not measurable — the O(n²) pass plus per-record sequential embedding is
  infeasible in a practical window.

The cheaper embedding path does **not** obviate #49 entity-keying; if anything it is the
clearest evidence yet *for* #49's design constraints. #49 explicitly mandates **passive
normalized facets** and **"No automatic entity merge."** This experiment shows exactly why:
automatic embedding-based merging at this corpus's near-duplicate density is actively
dangerous — it removes the very records search needs. A passive entity facet that
*disambiguates without merging* is the correct shape; an auto-merging semantic pass is not.
